It's time for the LEGAL side of the problem:

What laws have been passed to counteract global warming and what future laws could be passed?


What is currently regulated and what laws regulate them?

  1. Construction

    The National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) states that the environment has to be accounted for in any major project.
    Furthermore, the Rivers and Harbors Appropriation Act of 1899 prohibits the construction of dams, bridges or other structures without government approval.

  2. Hunting/Fishing/Poaching

    The Endangered species act prohibits the hunting of "threatened" species and funded their protection.

  3. Waste Management and Disposal:

    The Resource Conservation and Recovery Act gave the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) the ability to oversee any hazardous waste during "generation, transportation, treatment, storage, and disposal".

  4. Environmental Cleanup (e.g. Oil spills):

    The Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation, and Liability Act (Superfund / CERCLA) funded the cleanup of hazardous waste and gave the EPA the ability to hold the people who produced the waste responsible.

  5. Air Quality:

    The Clean Air Act makes the EPA responsible for preserving the ozone layer and set standards for the amount of pollution allowed in air.

  6. Water Quality:

    The Clean Water Act regulates the amount of pollution that is allowed into waterways and made illegal the dumping of pollution into water without permits. It also funded the contruction of water and sewage treatment plants.

What future laws could be passed?

  1. Limit the amount of Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere!

    There are laws about air pollution, but not greenhouse gases! Hold car companies responsible for the carbon dioxide they produce.

  2. A Carbon Tax?

    Tax people for the amount of greenhouse gases they produce, creating an incentive to go green.

  3. Funding for renewable energy

    Make it easier for companies and people to switch away from greenhouse gas-producing energy sources.
